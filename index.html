Rawalpindi Edition
Tuesday, July 5, 1977
Price: 50 Paisa

Army Overthrows
Bhutto's Government

"The situation is completely calm; Martial Law has been imposed across the country."

Zulfikar Ali Bhutto taken into protective custody.

Arrests Made

Several federal ministers and PPP leaders have been detained at Sihala Rest House for their own safety.

Assemblies Dissolved

RAWALPINDI: The Chief of Army Staff, General Zia-ul-Haq, has taken over the administration of the country. A proclamation issued early this morning states that the Constitution has been held in abeyance, and the National and Provincial Assemblies have been dissolved.

The General announced that this step was unavoidable to save the country from civil war. "The Army has no political ambitions," he stated, promising free and fair elections within 90 days.

  • Constitution suspended.
  • Civil courts continue to function.
  • Political activities banned temporarily.

"Operation Fair Play"

The operation commenced at midnight. Troops have moved into key installations. No resistance has been reported from any part of the country.
